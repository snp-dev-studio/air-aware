👥 Advisory by Group
📊 AQI Level Health Guide
0–50 Good
Air quality is satisfactory. No health risk. Ideal for all outdoor activities including running, cycling, and sports.
✅ All groups can go outdoors freely. No precautions needed.
51–100 Moderate
Acceptable air quality. A small number of unusually sensitive people may experience minor effects.
⚠️ Sensitive individuals: limit prolonged outdoor exertion.
101–150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
Children, elderly, and people with lung or heart disease should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion.
⚠️ Sensitive groups: stay indoors. General public: light activity OK.
151–200 Unhealthy
Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Members of sensitive groups may experience more serious effects.
🔴 Everyone: avoid prolonged outdoor exertion. Wear N95 mask outdoors.
201–300 Very Unhealthy
Health alert! Everyone may experience serious health effects. Avoid all outdoor activities.
🚨 Stay indoors. Keep windows closed. Use air purifier. N95 mandatory.
301–500 Hazardous
Emergency conditions. The entire population is likely to be affected. Serious risk of death for prolonged exposure.
☠️ Do NOT go outside. Seal doors/windows. Seek medical help if symptoms occur.

💡 General Health Tips
🌱

Add Indoor Plants

Spider plants, peace lilies, and snake plants naturally filter indoor air by absorbing harmful pollutants like benzene and formaldehyde.

🔧

Use HEPA Air Purifier

A HEPA air purifier can remove up to 99.97% of airborne particles including PM2.5 from your home. Run it in your bedroom at night.

🪟

Smart Ventilation

Open windows only when outdoor AQI is below 50. On bad air days, keep windows and doors closed to prevent pollution from entering.

🏃

Exercise Timing

Morning hours (5–8 AM) typically have lower AQI. Avoid exercising outdoors during 12 PM–4 PM when pollution peaks in most cities.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water on high pollution days. Proper hydration helps your body flush out toxins and supports respiratory health.

🥗

Anti-Pollution Diet

Foods rich in Vitamin C (oranges, amla), omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants strengthen your lungs and reduce inflammation from pollution exposure.

🛡️

Stay Protected

Always check the AQI before planning outdoor activities. When AQI exceeds 150, limit time outdoors and use protective masks. Your health is always the priority.
